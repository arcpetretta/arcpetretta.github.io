    • Stereotype threat describes the situation in which people feel that they could be potentially stereotyped / "confirm a negative stereotype about their social group."

    I've been fortunate enough that I've never really been subjected to negative stereotypes. I can only think of one specific instance where I was stereotyped but it pales in comparison to what some people have to deal with all their lives. With that said, I can remember the emotions I felt in that moment. At first I was surprised and kind of taken back that someone could say what they did. I thought it was a joke at first and then when I realized that this particular individual was being serious I felt so angry. I ended up just leaving that situation and rarely spoke to that person after that conversation.

    It wasn't until the next day that I was able to reflect on what happened with a clear head. I couldn't believe how mad I got over what was ultimately a stupid stereotype that I know to be untrue. But wow, I can totally see how people could get violent or alter their behavior in general as a result of negative stereotypes / discrimination. I mean for me I get mad because of stereotypes attached to my identity but that's about it. People of color and gay/trans people get it way worse... I those people are literally dying because of some of the stereotypes attached to them and it's apalling.

    Stereotypes as a whole are awkward to deal with. I think the best thing to do is address them when they come up. There have been times where I've had to step between a friend and a stranger because said stranger was being racist. Racism is messed up and that's putting it lightly but for better or worse sometimes it's just better to walk away from the situation. I know that may seem like the weak thing to do but with the way our society is sometimes it's just prudent to walk away.

    That's pretty much the extent of my experiences. I've been luckier than most and that's a blessing in itself. "Lucky" probably isn't the right word... There is inherent privilege that I have because of my skin color in this country. I'm not proud of it but it's outside of my control. All I can do is try to help out those who haven't been as fortunate as myself.
